ViLi: Special Needs' Awareness Platform

Vibrant Life

Nearly one fifth of all Americans experience a physical, sensory or intellectual disability that – unaccommodated – makes it difficult to participate in society. The problem is that local businesses often feature sensory stimuli – which is poorly documented online – that can trigger episodic responses without prior knowledge. This often results in the exclusion of disabled customers even in their own neighborhoods. To accommodate this community, my co-founder and I are crowdsourcing reviews of stores based on criteria relating to accessibility (colors, staff accommodation, hearing/visual-impairment experiences, Parkinson's accomodation etc.).

The goal of MyViLi.com is to use this open source data to allow special needs customers to better navigate their local markets. As of September 2020, we have received $5,000 of funds from MIT Sandbox to pursue this venture. In November 2019, my team and I were nominated as a finalist in the MIT 100K Pitch competition, where I presented the product to hundreds of venture capitalists and entrepreneurs looking to invest in small startup ideas.

Tutle Pond: Caltech Course Picker

Caltech Course Picker

When Covid-19 forced all undergraduate students to work from home, it became necessary to provide more up to date software for students to stay organized and on top of their courses. One problem with scheduling at Caltech is that, as a small school, class times can rapidly change as professors try to accomodate as many students (now in different time zones) as possible. Not all of these time changes are reported, resulting in conflicting information found on the class website, the registar's office, and the department's listings. In response to this confusion, I decided to code up a course picker web application that allows students to check/report errors in current class times, and maintain the most up-to-date schedules that they can follow from home.

The code for the website (Turtle-Pond.com) is written in HTML, CSS, and Javascript. The class information (names, times, prerequisites, term offered, etc) was compiled with python's Selenium package and is accessed from a JSON database. As of September 2020, I am the sole contributor to the code and the domain/hosting fees.

Mind Network: Online Physics Education Platform

Mind Network

Education is a powerful tool to combat fear of nuclear power and train critical thinking minds to question broad based unscientific claims. The problem is that at some point if a student wants to study advanced works, material is scarce and not well explained (if at all) outside of scientific literature. To allow for greater reach, I am developing a website (www.MindNetwork.us) to explain upper level courses to the general public by creating visually stimulating material to guide the user through the mathmatical formulas. As of Septemeber 2020, I have completed my course on quantum mechanics and developing further material for nuclear fusion and special relativity.

In developing this platform, I applied to and received $1000 from BetterMIT as well as $1000 from MIT’s Sandbox program. I additionally pursued further outreach by holding sessions at the MIT Campus Preview Weekend, MIT Innovation Initiative, and MIT’s Schwarzman College of Computing opening to gain traction for the product among my local community.

Augmedic: Transforming Emergency Care

Augmedic

As a part of an international competition in Zurich, Switzerland, I placed second collaborating with 4 MIT students to create a safer ambulance system using AI technology. Focusing on the medical side, I helped outline a workflow chart dictating the operations a computer should follow as a patient enters the ambulance. Following down the path, the system automatically evaluates key vital signs, sends them to the hospital, and provides advice to the medic on the status of the patient ("15% of the time the patient is drunk. You should check alcohol content“, "55% of the time the patient is having a stroke"). Founding our startup – Augmedic – we developed an integrative technology to improve the safety and efficiency of medical transport by combining artificial intelligence with biosensors to provide immediate feedback on minute patient fluctuations. Impressed with my leadership capacity, I was invited back the following year as the US facilitator for the competition.

About Me

My current research interest lies in the field of space medicine. I believe that the interdisciplinary work between physics and medicine is essential to progress our current medical equipment and progression of humanity in space. These interests include particle shielding, imaging technology, as well as radiation therapy. For my background, I have specifically worked on developing superconducting cyclotrons for proton therapy (MIT Plasma Science and Fusion Center), culturing radiation-absorbing nanoparticles to sterilize microbes in space (NASA AMES), and analyzing genomes of bacteria onboard the ISS (NASA JPL).

I am also interested in entrepreneurship. While the theoretical work of my studies excites me, what really drives me to continue is the opportunity to use my knowledge to make a real impact on the world today. Through my startups (Augmedic, Mind Network, Vili, etc), I hope to apply what I learned directly to my community.

Nonetheless, I am always open to learn new material because I fervently believe that all knowledge can and is indispensable in the medical field today. To broaden my education outside of the classroom, I have pursued biological research at the National Institutes of Health (NIH), biochemistry research at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and nuclear research at MIT’s Laboratory for Nuclear Science. I am very eager to continue my research, to continue learning and developing a broad, in depth, range of skills.
